Diego Correa Barahona

Diego is a PhD Candidate in the Department of Civil and Urban Engineering at New York University Tandon School of Engineering, and a master student in the Master of City and Regional Planning Program at Edward J. Bloustein School of Planning and Public Policy at Rutgers University. He has participated in transportation planning and design workshops in the United States, and Ecuador.

Diego has researched emerging bus rapid transit systems in Latin America, and United States and their impacts on the cities. His research and interests, focuses on bus rapid transit corridors BRT, Intelligent Transportation Systems, Traffic Simulation and Modeling, Transportation Planning, Traffic and, Transit Operation and Management, and Transportation Economics.

Diego received his Bachelor Degree in Civil Engineering from University of Cuenca in 2004, and Master Degree in Transportation Engineering from Pontifical Catholic University of Ecuador in 2012. He also received his M.Sc. in Civil Engineering, and a Graduate Certification in Transportation Studies form Edward J. Bloustein School of Planning and Public Policy from Rutgers University in 2014. In addition to riding buses and trains, he enjoys walking and bicycling.
